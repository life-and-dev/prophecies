# Into Your Hand I Commit My Spirit

> **Fulfillment Category:** Misunderstood Context
>
> This is David's personal prayer for deliverance from enemies, requesting rescue and asking not to be shamed. Jesus quoted one line while dying, but the context and requests in the psalm don't match Jesus' situation—David asks for speedy rescue, mentions being in a besieged city, and describes threats irrelevant to crucifixion.

## The Prophecy

Psalm 31 is David's prayer of trust amid persecution:

> In you, O LORD, do I take refuge; let me never be put to shame; in your righteousness deliver me! Incline your ear to me; rescue me speedily! Be a rock of refuge for me, a strong fortress to save me!
>
> **Into your hand I commit my spirit; you have redeemed me, O LORD, faithful God.** I hate those who pay regard to worthless idols, but I trust in the LORD.
>
> I will rejoice and be glad in your steadfast love, because you have seen my affliction; you have known the distress of my soul, and you have not delivered me into the hand of the enemy; you have set my feet in a broad place.
>
> Be gracious to me, O LORD, for I am in distress; my eye is wasted from grief; my soul and my body also... But I trust in you, O LORD; I say, "You are my God." My times are in your hand; rescue me from the hand of my enemies and from my persecutors!
>
> How abundant is your goodness, which you have stored up for those who fear you and worked for those who take refuge in you, in the sight of the children of mankind! In the cover of your presence you hide them from the plots of men; you store them in your shelter from the strife of tongues. **Blessed be the LORD, for he has wondrously shown his steadfast love to me when I was in a besieged city.** — Psalm 31:1-21 (selected verses, ESV)

## Christian Interpretation

Jesus quoted from this psalm while dying on the cross:

> Then Jesus, calling out with a loud voice, said, "**Father, into your hands I commit my spirit!**" And having said this he breathed his last. — Luke 23:46 (ESV)

Christians interpret this as:
- **Prophetic fulfillment** - Jesus consciously fulfilling David's words
- **Trust in God** - Jesus' final act of faith
- **Committing spirit** - Jesus' willing surrender to God
- **Redemption** - "You have redeemed me" points to Jesus' redemptive death

The psalm is understood as prophesying the Messiah's death in trusting submission to God.

## Original Context

### Why This Is Misunderstood

1. **Jesus quoted only one line**: Jesus used one phrase from the psalm ("into your hands I commit my spirit"), but quoting a single line doesn't make the entire psalm prophetic about him. People often quote Scriptures that express their current feelings without claiming the entire passage prophesies their situation.

2. **David requests not to be shamed**: The psalm begins, "let me never be put to shame" (v. 1). Jesus was:
   - Mocked by soldiers (Matthew 27:27-31)
   - Publicly stripped naked
   - Crucified between criminals
   - Taunted while dying (Matthew 27:39-44)

   Jesus experienced profound shame, contrary to David's request.

3. **David asks for speedy rescue**: Verse 2 says "rescue me **speedily**!" David wanted quick deliverance from his enemies. Jesus was:
   - Not rescued from crucifixion
   - Died after hours of suffering
   - Not delivered from his enemies' hands

4. **David mentions being in a "besieged city"**: Verse 21 says, "when I was in a **besieged city**." This describes military siege warfare. Jesus was:
   - Not in a besieged city
   - Crucified outside Jerusalem
   - His situation had nothing to do with siege warfare

5. **David prays to be hidden from enemies' plots**: Verses 19-20 say, "In the cover of your presence you hide them from the plots of men; you store them in your shelter from the strife of tongues." David asks God to hide him from conspiracies. Jesus was:
   - Not hidden from conspiracies
   - Captured by those plotting against him
   - Publicly tried and executed according to their plots

6. **David describes past deliverance**: Verse 8 says, "you have not delivered me into the hand of the enemy; you have set my feet in a broad place." David thanks God for past rescue. Jesus was:
   - Delivered into his enemies' hands
   - Not given freedom ("broad place") but was arrested, tried, and killed

7. **The entire psalm is David's personal prayer**: The superscription reads "To the choirmaster. A Psalm of David." This is David's prayer amid his own persecution (possibly from Saul or Absalom). The details fit David's historical circumstances, not Jesus' crucifixion.

### Assessment

Psalm 31 is David's personal prayer for deliverance from enemies, asking for quick rescue, protection from shame, and relief from persecution. Jesus quoted one line expressing trust in God, but the psalm's overall context and specific requests don't match Jesus' experience. David asks not to be shamed (Jesus was shamed), to be rescued speedily (Jesus was not rescued), and describes being in a besieged city (Jesus was not).

Quoting a psalm doesn't make it prophetic. People often quote Scripture that expresses their feelings without implying the entire passage prophesies them. Jesus used David's words of trust, but that doesn't transform David's personal prayer into a messianic prophecy.

The Christian interpretation depends on:
- Treating one quoted line as making the entire psalm prophetic
- Ignoring the psalm's specific details that contradict Jesus' experience
- Overlooking that David's request for deliverance from shame and speedy rescue were not granted to Jesus
- Assuming any Scripture Jesus quoted must be about him prophetically rather than him using existing prayers

**Strength of Fulfillment**: Misunderstood Context. The passage is David's personal prayer for deliverance, with details that don't match Jesus' crucifixion. Jesus quoted one line expressing trust, not claiming the psalm prophesied him.
