# Covenant with My Chosen, David

> **Fulfillment Category:** Misunderstood Context
>
> This psalm recounts God's covenant with David, addressed to David with conditions about "his sons." Jesus had no biological children, making the dynastic succession language inapplicable. The covenant concerns David's descendants collectively, not exclusively one future Messiah.

## The Prophecy

Psalm 89 is a lengthy meditation on God's covenant with David:

> I will sing of the steadfast love of the LORD, forever; with my mouth I will make known your faithfulness to all generations... You have said, "I have made a covenant with my chosen one; **I have sworn to David my servant: 'I will establish your offspring forever, and build your throne for all generations.'"**
>
> **Of old you spoke in a vision to your godly one, and said: "I have granted help to one who is mighty; I have exalted one chosen from the people. I have found David, my servant; with my holy oil I have anointed him, so that my hand shall be established with him; my arm also shall strengthen him..."**
>
> **He shall cry to me, 'You are my Father, my God, and the Rock of my salvation.' And I will make him the firstborn, the highest of the kings of the earth.** My steadfast love I will keep for him forever, and my covenant will stand firm for him. **I will establish his offspring forever, and his throne as the days of the heavens.**
>
> **If his sons forsake my law and do not walk according to my rules, if they violate my statutes and do not keep my commandments, then I will punish their transgression with the rod and their iniquity with stripes, but I will not remove from him my steadfast love or be false to my faithfulness.** I will not violate my covenant or alter the word that went forth from my lips. Once for all I have sworn by my holiness; I will not lie to David. His offspring shall endure forever, his throne as long as the sun before me. — Psalm 89:1-36 (selected verses, ESV)

## Christian Interpretation

Christians interpret this psalm as referring to Jesus because:
- **"Your offspring forever"** - Jesus as David's ultimate descendant
- **"I will make him the firstborn"** - Jesus as the preeminent One
- **"Highest of the kings of the earth"** - Jesus' supreme authority
- **"You are my Father"** - Jesus' unique sonship
- **Eternal throne** - Jesus' everlasting kingdom

The psalm is understood as prophesying the Messiah's eternal reign as David's descendant.

## Original Context

### Why This Is Misunderstood

1. **The covenant is addressed to David about his descendants**: God says:
   - "I have sworn to **David my servant**"
   - "I will establish **your offspring** forever"
   - "**His sons**" (v. 30)

   This is God's covenant with David about his lineage, not a specific prophecy about one individual Messiah.

2. **The covenant has conditions for "his sons"**: Verses 30-32 say:
   > **If his sons forsake my law** and do not walk according to my rules, if they violate my statutes and do not keep my commandments, **then I will punish their transgression** with the rod and their iniquity with stripes.

   The covenant includes:
   - Conditional obedience required from David's sons
   - Punishment for disobedience
   - Multiple descendants ("sons" plural)

   This describes an ongoing dynasty with multiple generations, not one individual.

3. **Jesus had no biological children**: The psalm speaks repeatedly about:
   - "His offspring" (descendants)
   - "His sons"
   - Establishing "his throne for all generations"

   Jesus had no biological children to establish a throne or dynasty. The language assumes multiple generations of descendants.

4. **The psalm describes David's historical sons**: The conditional language ("if his sons forsake my law... I will punish their transgression") describes what actually happened:
   - Solomon sinned, and the kingdom was divided (1 Kings 11)
   - Many of David's descendants were wicked and punished
   - The Davidic dynasty eventually ended with the Babylonian exile

   This is about David's actual sons and descendants, not exclusively about Jesus.

5. **"Firstborn" and "highest of the kings" refer to status among David's line**: Verse 27: "I will make him the firstborn, the highest of the kings of the earth." In context:
   - David was not literally God's firstborn
   - "Firstborn" means preeminence or favored status (like Ephraim in Jeremiah 31:9)
   - "Highest of the kings" means David/his dynasty would be exalted above other earthly kingdoms

   This is royal court language about David's dynasty, not prophecy of divine Messiah.

6. **The latter part of the psalm laments the covenant's apparent failure**: Verses 38-51 express anguish that God seems to have broken the covenant:
   > But now you have cast off and rejected; you are full of wrath against your anointed. You have renounced the covenant with your servant; you have defiled his crown in the dust. (vv. 38-39)

   The psalmist questions why the Davidic kingdom has fallen despite God's promises. This reflects historical reality—the dynasty's collapse—not a prophecy awaiting fulfillment in Jesus.

7. **Christians already have a more explicit Davidic covenant prophecy**: 2 Samuel 7:12-16 is the primary Davidic covenant passage and is already included in the messianic prophecy list as "The Davidic Covenant." Psalm 89 simply recounts that same covenant—it's not an additional prophecy but a poetic reflection on the existing covenant.

### Assessment

Psalm 89 is a poetic meditation on God's covenant with David recorded in 2 Samuel 7. It reflects on the promises God made to establish David's dynasty forever and the conditions attached to those promises (his sons must obey, or face punishment). The latter part of the psalm laments that the covenant seems broken because of the Davidic kingdom's collapse.

The psalm addresses:
- David and his many descendants ("his sons")
- An ongoing dynasty through multiple generations
- Conditional obedience and punishment for disobedience
- Historical reality of David's descendants' sins and punishment

The Christian interpretation depends on:
- Ignoring "his sons" (plural) and conditional language about multiple descendants
- Overlooking that Jesus had no biological children to establish a dynasty
- Reading the psalm as exclusively about one future individual rather than an ongoing dynasty
- Extracting phrases about eternal throne while ignoring the surrounding context about David's actual sons
- Treating a poetic reflection on an existing covenant (2 Samuel 7) as a separate additional prophecy

**Strength of Fulfillment**: Misunderstood Context. The passage poetically recounts God's covenant with David about his ongoing dynasty ("his sons"), including conditional obedience and punishment for disobedience, not exclusively prophesying one future Messiah.
