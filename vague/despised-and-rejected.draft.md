# Despised and Rejected

## The Prophecy

> **He was despised and rejected by men, a man of sorrows, and acquainted with grief**; and as one from whom men hide their faces he was despised, and we esteemed him not. Surely **he has borne our griefs and carried our sorrows**; yet we esteemed him stricken, smitten by God, and afflicted. — Isaiah 53:3-4 (ESV)

Social rejection and suffering on behalf of others.

## Fulfillment in Jesus

His own disciples abandomed Jesus:

> “Do you think that I cannot appeal to my Father, and he will at once send me more than twelve legions of angels? But how then should **the Scriptures be fulfilled**, that it must be so?” 
>
> At that hour Jesus said to the crowds, “**Have you come out as against a robber, with swords and clubs to capture me?** Day after day I sat in the temple teaching, and you did not seize me. But all this has taken place that **the Scriptures of the prophets might be fulfilled.**” 
>
> Then **all the disciples left him and fled**.
>
> — Matthew 26:53-56 (ESV)

Even his brothers didn't believe:

> For not **even his brothers** believed in him. — John 7:5 (ESV)

Likewise many Jews rejected Jesus:

> He came to his own, and **his own people did not receive him**. — John 1:11 (ESV)

> So **the Jews** sought all the more **to kill him**, because not only was he breaking the Sabbath, but he was even calling God his own Father, making himself equal with God. — John 5:18 (ESV)

## Christian Perspective

Jesus experienced complete rejection: by religious leaders, by his nation, even by family members initially. This profound rejection fulfills the prophecy's description of being "despised and rejected by men."

## Assessment

The historical record clearly documents Jesus' rejection by Jewish leaders, his own people, and even temporary abandonment by disciples. The progression from rejection to crucifixion matches the prophecy's description, however, the prophecy itself is not specific enough to be unique to Jesus only. Many great prophets had be rejected since Isaiah wrote this prophecy.
